Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Target Simulation and Reconstruction

Personal Project · Individual · 2025 · Spaceborne Radar Remote Systems

Context

Personal MATLAB-based simulation project developed to deepen theoretical understanding from the courses Radar Systems Engineering and Spaceborne Radar Remote Sensing. The project focused on modeling SAR signal generation and target reconstruction from first principles.

Engineering Problem

Simulate the radar return from a point target using chirp waveforms and reconstruct the target image through matched filtering and convolution-based processing. The primary challenges included:
- Accurately modeling the point target response as a function of range
- Incorporating radar platform motion and its Doppler frequency effects
- Accounting for received power variation with azimuth angle
- Achieving proper range and azimuth compression

My Contribution

  • Modeled transmitted linear FM chirp waveform
  • Simulated target reflection considering propagation delay and motion
  • Implemented convolution-based matched filtering
  • Performed range compression
  • Implemented azimuth processing and analyzed resolution limitations
  • Evaluated resolution trade-offs between range and azimuth domains

Tools & Methods

MATLAB Linear FM signal modeling Matched filtering Range and azimuth compression algorithms SAR

Results

Successful range compression using matched filtering. Partial azimuth reconstruction achieved. Identified resolution degradation due to motion modeling and Doppler approximation constraints. Evaluated sensitivity of azimuth resolution to platform velocity assumptions

What I Learned

Developed a stronger understanding of SAR signal processing fundamentals, particularly the role of Doppler processing and motion modeling in azimuth resolution. Gained practical insight into the sensitivity of SAR reconstruction to modeling assumptions.
