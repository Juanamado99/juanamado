Patch Antenna Array (DoSis)

Student Job in Research (KIT) · Team of 5 PhD · 2025 · Antenna Systems in Radar + Integrated Circuit Research Environment

Context

Research-oriented multidisciplinary job focused on the design and optimization of a compact patch antenna array for radar applications with a PhD team.

Engineering Problem

Design an antenna array given the existing PCB height, materials and port width to radiate 45º against the radar, 7dBi or greater of gain and have a coupling factor lower than 30dB.

My Contribution

  • Design modeling and simulation
  • Parameter sweeps and geometry optimization
  • Design and test decoupling structures
  • Radiation pattern, impedance matching, gain and bandwidth analysis

Tools & Methods

CST Studio Suite Book: Antenna Theory - Balanis

Results

Achieved 11.5 dBi gain. Reached -20dB S21 parameter (between input and output port) which still needs to be improved by 10dB. Improved impedance matching to maximize chip output signal with a double layer PCB.

What I Learned

Improved understanding of behavior in strip lines waveguides and efficiency using optimization techniques. Wider knowledge on radiation patterns and CST Studio Suite results for antenna design.
