Design and Optimization of Dielectric Waveguide Structures and Transitions in PCB

Bachelor's Thesis · Supervised by Mario Faliero · 2025 · RF Systems

Context

Bachelor’s thesis developed in collaboration with a doctoral research team. Individual responsibility for the electromagnetic design of three transmission lines and six transitions compatible with PCB manufacturing.

Engineering Problem

Design, simulate and build low-loss dielectric waveguide structures suitable for PCB fabrication, ensuring minimal insertion loss, good impedance matching, and smooth transitions between guiding structures at high frequencies.

My Contribution

  • Full-wave EM modeling and simulation
  • Parametric sweeps and geometry optimization
  • Design of 3 dielectric guiding structures
  • Design and tuning of 6 transitions between structures
  • Analysis of S-parameters and field distributions
  • Build and measurement of the components

Tools & Methods

CST Studio Suite MATLAB VNA (Vector Network Analyser) PCB print design

Results

Achieved low insertion loss and acceptable return loss within target frequency band (21-30GHz). Demonstrated manufacturable PCB-compatible dielectric guiding solution. Validated transition performance with consistent impedance matching

What I Learned

Deepened understanding of high-frequency dielectric propagation, transition design, and simulation-driven optimization under fabrication constraints.
